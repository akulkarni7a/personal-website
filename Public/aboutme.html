Hi, I'm AK.

I once tried teaching myself the Saxophone by watching YouTube videos – if you need somebody to play Hot Cross Buns at your wedding, I’m your guy. In high school, as a way to prove to myself that you lose nothing by trying, I did a 10 minute standup comedy set. In college, I taught myself advanced Excel formulas to help understand the NFL’s Collective Bargaining Agreement.

Each of those experiences taught me a few important things:

  1.  I alone am responsible for the opportunities in my life - If there’s something I’m passionate about, the only thing that will ever prevent me from accomplishing it is me

  2.  Learning is fun – whether it’s learning a new coding language or “Old MacDonald” on the Saxophone, developing myself is a worthy – and enjoyable task

  3. True success comes in collaboration – there’s only so much one person can do. In my career I’ve been lucky enough to be a part of high performing teams that all trust and respect each other and it has been a completely transformational experience. The diversity of thought and accountability that comes from a team cannot be replicated.

On that note, I love building things – if you’re interested in working together, let me know! Examples of my coding projects can be found in “Portfolio.” My resume can be found below.
